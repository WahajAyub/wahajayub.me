About this feed

A research instrument,
not a recommendation engine.

The radar is designed to surface a small number of papers that connect directly to my work on topological photovoltaic response, quantum geometry, symmetry, and the mathematical structure of band topology.

01

The research profile

Papers receive priority when they connect multiple parts of this research program.

  • Shift, injection, impurity-assisted, and ballistic photocurrent
  • Quantum metric, Berry curvature, and nonlinear response
  • Noncentrosymmetric topological materials
  • Hopf, Hopf-Euler, and crystalline Hopf phases
  • Delicate, unstable, and multigap invariants
  • Homotopy, characteristic classes, and mathematical condensed matter
02

What gets highlighted

A topological material receives a visible noncentrosymmetric highlight only when broken bulk inversion symmetry is supported by the supplied evidence. Surface or interface inversion breaking is recorded separately.

Papers connecting Hopf or delicate invariants to optical response, shift current, or quantum geometry receive the highest interdisciplinary priority.

03

Mechanisms stay distinct

The classifier does not use “shift current” as a synonym for the full bulk photovoltaic effect. Intrinsic shift, injection, ballistic, impurity-assisted, excitation, and recombination contributions are labeled separately whenever the abstract supports that distinction.

04

Token-efficient by design

Deterministic filters and cached identifiers remove noise before any model call. A small model ranks titles and abstracts; only selected papers receive a detailed summary and research-relevance analysis.

Transparency

What the summaries mean

Summaries paraphrase source abstracts and selected accessible text. They are not peer review, endorsement, or a substitute for reading the paper. When evidence is incomplete, the feed should say so rather than infer a symmetry, invariant, or mechanism.

“Relevance to my research” is an editorial bridge: it identifies a concrete mechanism, material, mathematical tool, benchmark, or open question that could inform my work.
